Challenge: Widgets

1) What you think this system does ?

For the given data, the schema is probably of a wholesale store selling pesticides. The system has information on pricing of the widget being sold and its inventory details.
It also had details of customers as well as suppliers and the quantity. The data itself seems transactional on purchases and current inventory of the widget item

2) What you feel would be a reasonable database structure for the data and a reasonable architecture for the system ?

I would apply Third Normal Form (3NF) considering the relation between customer, supplier, warehouse, product, pricing and inventory tables to normalize the data and to reduce duplication of data.
The pricing can further be categorized based on widget-supplier availability and inventory can be setup by daily tracking of widgets by supplier and warehouse.
From left to right, I created look ups of widgets, customers, widget-supplier combination (multiple suppliers may be selling a particular widget), widget-packaging and warehouse.
Moreover, the qty and min_qty columns can also be further setup into something that can lead to inventory tracking.


3) Any questions or concerns you have regarding this dataset/system that might need to be answered before establishing an ideal database/solution for such a system.

If the given data is about customer purchases then I think it is missing timestamps, is that so ? What are the price and cost columns ? Are these price the customer paid and cost to company to source the widget ? How often is the inventory updated ? This may help the customer see inventory live through the system.
